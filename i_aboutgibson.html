What is The Grand Gibson Hotel?

As the name suggests it is a hotel, a grand hotel. It’s not a hotel in rea-life but one that has served a purpose in two of my works. To fully understand what the Grand Gibson is all about, it’s best to start at the beginning, the name.

I chose the word “grand” to imply the size, scale, and quality of the hotel. Once the project(s) were to be completed, the name would be fitting. Gibson comes from the name William Gibson, an American Canadian writer and essayist credited as the pioneer of one of my favorite subgenres “Cyberpunk.”

The name was chosen because the Grand Gibson Hotel was to be the crown jewel of a 2120 cyberpunk iteration of Chicago, Illinois. As the narrative told have it, by the time The Grand Gibson Hotel was finished in 2118, it effectively but the Sheraton Grand Chicago, Omni Chicago Hotel, and Hilton Chicago to shame.

The first iteration of The Gibson served a platform for me and a follow classmate to showcase what we were learning while we pursued our education at the Art Institute of California – North Hollywood. She wanted to showcase her art and substance materials and I needed a good showcase to practice my visual storytelling. Thus, Project Gibson was born.

Over a span 6 – 7 weeks, my colleague and I held meetings over Skype and met in person to discuss designs and construct of the project. The hotel was to be modeled using modular design and built in Unreal Engine 4. Enthusiasm for the project eventually simmered and product came slowed as class projects, school, and work became an all-consuming priority. My colleague graduated which ultimately killed production and any plans we had at thepime for the hotel but to this day I remain the admin for the official Facebook that was created to house the reference images, color palettes, sketches, and all sorts of materials (like some found here on this page) used during pre-production.

The second iteration of The Grand Gibson wasn’t planned for construct but rather served as an essential setting for a narrative I revisited after graduating. The story needed a grand setting for the climax to take place and after thumbing through a few of my old works, I decided the Gibson was the perfect place. That narrative is still being worked and written as an episodic series. If you’re interested, click here. Today, The Grand Gibson Hotel serves as a template for this website’s structure, providing color schemes, content, and a little atmosphere in remembrance of what the Gibson was to be.
